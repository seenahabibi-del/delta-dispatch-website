<section id="education">

  <div class="section-tag">Understanding BESS</div>

  <h2>The Technology, The Market, The Moment</h2>

  <p>Battery Energy Storage Systems represent one of the most significant infrastructure opportunities of the energy transition. For those new to the sector, we offer a clear, authoritative introduction.</p>


  <div class="edu-grid">

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>What Is a BESS?</h3>

      <p>A Battery Energy Storage System is a large-scale installation of lithium-ion or alternative chemistry battery modules, power conversion equipment, and control systems that can absorb, store, and discharge electricity at scale.</p>

      <p>Modern grid-scale BESS units range from single-digit megawatt-hours (MWh) for smaller projects to multi-gigawatt-hour installations serving national grid operators. They can respond to grid signals in milliseconds — far faster than any thermal generation asset.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>What Is Curtailment?</h3>

      <p>Curtailment occurs when a renewable generator is instructed by the grid operator to reduce or cease output, despite being capable of generating electricity. This happens when local or national grid capacity cannot absorb available supply — a structural constraint that increases as renewable penetration grows.</p>

      <p>In Great Britain, wind farm operators receive constraint payments to curtail output, but this represents a fundamental inefficiency in the energy system. BESS resolves this by absorbing the excess energy at the point of generation.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>How Does BESS Generate Revenue?</h3>

      <p>BESS assets access multiple co-existing revenue streams: <strong style="color:var(--white)">Frequency Response Services</strong> (paid by National Grid ESO for rapid stabilisation of grid frequency), <strong style="color:var(--white)">Capacity Market Agreements</strong> (government-backed contracts for reliable capacity), <strong style="color:var(--white)">Wholesale Energy Arbitrage</strong> (buy low, sell high), and <strong style="color:var(--white)">Direct PPAs</strong> with corporate energy buyers such as AI data centres and EV fleet operators.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>Why AI and EV Demand Matters</h3>

      <p>The International Energy Agency forecasts that data centres, AI, and cryptocurrency could account for over 1,000 TWh of global electricity consumption annually by 2026 — comparable to Japan's entire electricity consumption today.</p>

      <p>Major technology companies have made legally binding commitments to 100% renewable energy procurement. They actively seek large-scale, verified clean energy storage solutions. Delta Dispatch's model is designed precisely to serve this demand with integrity and at scale.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>The Planning Landscape</h3>

      <p>Battery storage projects in England under 50MW fall under the Town and Country Planning Act, processed by Local Planning Authorities. Projects above 50MW are classified as Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects (NSIPs) and consented by the Planning Inspectorate.</p>

      <p>The UK Government has identified battery storage as a critical infrastructure priority. Policy support is strong, planning risk is manageable for well-located sites, and grid connection reform under Project RAPID is reducing connection timescales substantially.</p>

    </div>

    <div class="edu-card">

      <h3>Environmental Credentials</h3>

      <p>Delta Dispatch operates exclusively with 100% sustainable energy inputs. Every megawatt-hour stored and dispatched by our assets is traceable to a verified curtailed or surplus renewable source. We produce no carbon, consume no fossil fuels, and contribute directly to the decarbonisation of electricity-intensive sectors that have historically struggled to source credible green power.</p>

      <p>Our environmental credentials are available for audit and reporting by corporate partners and offtakers.</p>
